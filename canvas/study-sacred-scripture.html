Bible Study on the Nature of God

The Sacred Scriptures, also known as the Bible, are the written Word of God. They contain the story of God's revelation to humanity through the history of the Chosen People of Israel and the early Christian Church.

The Bible is a collection of 73 books - 46 in the Old Testament and 27 in the New Testament. The Old Testament tells the story of God's relationship with Israel before Christ. It includes books of law, history, wisdom, and prophecy. The New Testament contains the four Gospels which tell the story of Jesus's life, death, and resurrection, as well as letters from apostles to early Christian communities and the Book of Revelation.

Catholics consider the Bible to be divinely inspired - God worked through the human writers' talents and experiences to reveal Himself. The Bible teaches us about who God is, His love for us, and His plan for our salvation.

The Bible is central to Catholic faith. We proclaim readings from the Bible at every Mass. Studying and praying with Scripture helps us encounter God and grow deeper in our faith. I encourage you to spend time daily reading the Bible. One great way to do this is to read the daily Mass readings using the Laudate app. Immersing ourselves in Scripture will enrich our relationship with God and understanding of our faith.

Here are some select readings from the Bible that are pertinent to this week's lesson.  No need to read all of them.  Read those that seem interesting to you as time allows.  

  1. - Deuteronomy 6:4 - "Hear, O Israel: The Lord our God, the Lord is one." God is the one and only God.
  2. - Exodus 3:14 - "I AM WHO I AM." God reveals himself as the eternal, self-existent, unchanging Yahweh.
  3. - Isaiah 40:28 - "The LORD is the everlasting God, the Creator of the ends of the earth." God is eternal, all-powerful, and the Creator. 
  4. - Jeremiah 10:10 - "The LORD is the true God; he is the living God and the everlasting King." God is the one true God, everlasting King.
  5. - Psalm 90:2 - "From everlasting to everlasting you are God." God has no beginning and no end.
  6. - Psalm 145:3 - "Great is the LORD and most worthy of praise; his greatness no one can fathom." God is infinitely great beyond human comprehension. 
  7. - 1 John 4:8 - "God is love." The very nature of God is perfect, sacrificial, unconditional love.
  8. - Revelation 4:8 - "Holy, holy, holy is the Lord God Almighty, who was, and is, and is to come." God is absolutely holy.
  9. - Psalm 139:7-10 - God is omnipresent, everywhere at all times.
  10. - Psalm 147:5 - "Great is our Lord and mighty in power; his understanding has no limit." God is all-powerful and omniscient.
  11. - Isaiah 6:3 - "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD Almighty." God is thrice holy.
  12. - Psalm 115:3 - "Our God is in heaven; he does whatever pleases him." God is sovereign, free, and not constrained.
  13. - 1 Timothy 1:17 - "Now to the King eternal, immortal, invisible, the only God, be honor and glory forever and ever." God is eternal, immortal, invisible, and worthy of glory.
  14. - Psalm 102:27 - "You remain the same, and your years will never end." God never changes.
  15. - Psalm 18:30 - "As for God, his way is perfect." God is morally flawless. 
  16. - Romans 11:33 - "Oh, the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!" God's wisdom and knowledge are unfathomably deep.
  17. - Psalm 104:24 - "How many are your works, LORD! In wisdom you made them all." God created all things in wisdom.
  18. - Hebrews 4:13 - "Nothing in all creation is hidden from God’s sight. Everything is uncovered and laid bare before the eyes of him to whom we must give account." God is omniscient.
  19. - Deuteronomy 32:4 - “He is the Rock, his works are perfect, and all his ways are just. A faithful God who does no wrong, upright and just is he.” God is just and upright.
  20. - Isaiah 57:15 - “For this is what the high and exalted One says—he who lives forever, whose name is holy: I live in a high and holy place but also with the one who is contrite and lowly in spirit, to revive the spirit of the lowly and to revive the heart of the contrite.” God is holy, eternal, dwelling in heaven but near the humble and contrite.
