Good reading if you want more on the Sign of the Cross:

A Short History of the Sign of the Cross - WordOnFire.org

 

Quick summary of the article:

  • The sign of the cross originated in apostolic times as a baptismal prayer.
  • By the 3rd century, Christians used small crosses on their bodies and as blessings. 
  • In the 7th-8th centuries, large signs of the cross became popular to defend orthodoxy against heresy.
  • By the 9th century, Eastern Christians from right to left, Western did it both ways, but left to right became the tradition. 
  • Eventually the large left to right cross prevailed in the West, influenced by Benedictines.
  • Prayers with the sign varied over time to the current Trinitarian formula.
  • Today variation remains in how the cross is made, but it always opens Christians to God.

 

Other points to think about of the Sign of the Cross:

  • It is a prayer.
  • It can be a remembrance of our Baptism where we are Baptized in the name of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit.
  • It can also remember Jesus' command with authority to the Disciples to teach and to baptize in the name of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit.
  • It can be a visible sign of our devotion to God which God may work in the other.
  • It can be a way to bless yourself, your day, your children, your loved ones.
  • It can unite us with Christians for at least 1800 years!
  • It can mark us as a devoted child of God for persecution.  
  • It can let others know you are united to them in Faith.
