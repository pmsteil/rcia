Baptism Sacred Scripture Study

Read each passage from Sacred Scripture to learn more how Jesus instituted the Sacrament of Baptism and how the early church practiced it.

  1. Mark 1:9-11: This passage describes the baptism of Jesus by John the Baptist. It is the first time that baptism is mentioned in the New Testament, and it sets the stage for the understanding of baptism as a sacrament of initiation into the Christian faith.

  2. Matthew 28:18-20: This passage, known as the Great Commission, contains Jesus' command, WITH HIS AUTHORITY, to his apostles to "go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you." This passage is seen as the official institution of the sacrament of Baptism by Jesus.

  3. Acts 2:36-39: This passage describes the baptism of the first Christians on Pentecost. It is a powerful example of the transformative power of baptism, and it shows how baptism is a way of entering into the Christian community.

  4. Romans 6:1-11: This passage explains that baptism is a way of dying to sin and rising to new life in Christ. It is a powerful symbol of the believer's new identity in Christ.

  5. Galatians 3:26-29: This passage states that "all who have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ." This means that baptism is a way of being clothed with the righteousness of Christ, and it is a source of great hope for Christians.

  6. Colossians 2:9-12: This passage states that baptism is "the burial of the self with Christ...in the forgiveness of sins." This means that baptism is a way of washing away our sins and starting anew in Christ.

  7. Ephesians 4:1-5: This passage states that there is "one Lord, one faith, one baptism." This means that baptism is a unifying sacrament that unites all Christians into one body.

  8. 1 Peter 3:18-21: This passage states that baptism is "not the removal of dirt from the body but the appeal of a good conscience toward God, through the resurrection of Jesus Christ." This means that baptism is not just an external ritual, but it is also a way of cleansing our hearts and consciences.

  9. Acts 16:30-34: This passage describes the baptism of the Philippian jailer and his household. It is a powerful example of the transformative power of baptism, and it shows how baptism can lead to conversion to Christianity as well as the idea of Baptizing infants and children.  

  10. Acts 18:8: This passage states that "Crispus, the synagogue ruler, and his household all believed in the Lord and were baptized." This is another example of how baptism was a way of an entire family entering into the Christian community.

 

Confirmation Sacred Scripture Study

  1. Acts 2:38: Peter instructs the people to repent and be baptized, promising the gift of the Holy Spirit in exchange.

  2. Acts 8:14-17: The apostles in Jerusalem send Peter and John to Samaria to lay their hands on the Samaritans so that they can receive the Holy Spirit.

  3. Acts 19:5-6: Paul lays his hands on the believers in Ephesus, who receive the Holy Spirit and start speaking in tongues and prophesying.

  4. Hebrews 6:1-2: The author of Hebrews encourages the readers to move on to perfection, not laying again the foundation of repentance from dead works and of faith toward God, and of instruction about baptisms, and the laying on of hands, and the resurrection of the dead, and the eternal judgment.

  5. Ephesians 4:30: The author of Ephesians warns the readers not to grieve the Holy Spirit of God, in whom they were sealed for the day of redemption.

  6. 2 Corinthians 1:21-22: The author of 2 Corinthians assures the readers that it is God who establishes us in Christ and has anointed us, given us the seal, and the Spirit in our hearts as a guarantee.

  7. Romans 8:16-17: The author of Romans declares that the Spirit himself testifies with our spirit that we are God's children. If we are children, we are heirs, heirs of God and co-heirs with Christ.

  8. Galatians 3:26-27: The author of Galatians asserts that all of us are sons of God through faith in Christ Jesus, and that as many of us as were baptized into Christ have put on Christ.

  9. 1 Peter 3:21: The author of 1 Peter explains that baptism saves us, not the removal of dirt from the body but the appeal of a good conscience toward God, through the resurrection of Jesus Christ.

  10. Titus 3:5-6: The author of Titus declares that God saved us, not because of works done by us in righteousness, but according to his mercy, by the washing of regeneration and renewal of the Holy Spirit, whom he poured out on us abundantly, through Jesus Christ our Savior.
