Jesus starts a Church with Authority

This section highlights key Scripture references from the Gospels and the Book of Acts that demonstrate Jesus' establishment of the Church with divine authority. These passages show Jesus entrusting leadership roles to individuals like Peter, emphasizing the importance of confronting sin in the church, commissioning His disciples for the Great Commission, and forming the early Christian community.

Matthew

  • Matthew 16:18-19 - Jesus says he will build his church and gives Peter the keys of the kingdom and power to bind and loose
  • Matthew 18:15-20 - Instructions on confronting sin in the church and Jesus' presence where two or three gather
  • Matthew 28:18-20 - The Great Commission to make disciples and teach them all Jesus commanded

John

  • John 20:21-23 - Jesus commissions the disciples and gives them power to forgive sins

Acts

  • Acts 2:42-47 - Description of the early church devoted to apostles' teaching, fellowship, breaking bread, and prayer
  • Acts 6:1-7 - Choosing of deacons to serve the church
  • Acts 11:19-26 - Church formed in Antioch and disciples first called Christians
  • Acts 15:1-35 - The Jerusalem Council establishes doctrine for the church

Ephesians

  • Ephesians 1:22-23 - Christ is head over the church, his body
  • Ephesians 3:10 - Church displays the wisdom of God
  • Ephesians 4:11-16 - Gifted leaders equip the church for maturity and unity
  • Ephesians 5:25-27 - Christ loved the church and sanctifies her

1 Timothy

  • 1 Timothy 3:1-13 - Qualifications for overseers and deacons
  • 1 Timothy 3:14-16 - The church upholds the truth of Christ
  • 1 Timothy 4:13 - Scripture reading, teaching, preaching in the church
  • 1 Timothy 5:17 - Elders who direct the church well should be honored

2 Timothy

  • 2 Timothy 2:2 - Entrusting sound doctrine to faithful leaders to teach others
  • 2 Timothy 4:2-5 - Preach the word, correct false teaching in the church

Titus

  • Titus 1:5-9 - Appointing elders to teach sound doctrine and refute error

1 Peter

  • 1 Peter 5:1-5 - Elders shepherd the flock willingly and eagerly

2 Peter

  • 2 Peter 2:1-3 - Warning against false teachers bringing heresies into the church

Colossians

  • Colossians 1:18 - Christ is the head of the church
  • Colossians 1:24 - Paul's suffering for the sake of the church
  • Colossians 3:12-17 - Virtues for living as Christ's church

 

The Church as Teacher/Defender of His Truth

This section emphasizes the Church's role as the guardian and proclaimer of God's truth. It includes passages that underscore the Church's foundational role in upholding and transmitting the teachings of Jesus, with a special emphasis on the role of apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors, and teachers. The Church is presented as the pillar and foundation of the truth, built on the foundation of apostles and prophets, and instrumental in making God's wisdom known.

  • 1 Timothy 3:15 - The church is the pillar and foundation of the truth.
  • Ephesians 2:20 - The church is built on the foundation of the apostles and prophets, with Christ as the cornerstone.
  • Ephesians 3:10 - Through the church the manifold wisdom of God is made known.
  • Ephesians 4:11-13 - Christ gave the church apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers to equip the saints for ministry and building up the body.
  • 1 Corinthians 12:28 - God appointed in the church first apostles, second prophets, third teachers, then miracles, gifts of healing, helping, administrating, and different kinds of tongues.
  • Colossians 1:18 - Christ is the head of the body, the church.
  • 1 Timothy 6:20 - Timothy is to guard the truth entrusted to him, avoiding godless chatter and contradictions of false knowledge.
  • 2 Timothy 2:2 - The things Timothy heard from Paul he is to entrust to faithful people to teach others.
  • Titus 2:1 - Paul tells Titus to teach sound doctrine and instruct the church in righteous living.

 

The Church Hierarchy

This section focuses on the hierarchical structure within the Church, as depicted in the New Testament. The passages outline the appointment and qualifications of elders, bishops, and deacons, emphasizing the importance of leadership roles in shepherding and overseeing the Church. The structure outlined in these verses helps maintain order and spiritual guidance within the Christian community.

Acts

  • Acts 14:23 - Paul and Barnabas appoint elders in each church
  • Acts 20:17, 28 - Elders (bishops/overseers) shepherd and oversee the church

Philippians

  • Philippians 1:1 - Letter addressed to overseers/deacons along with the church

1 Timothy

  • 1 Timothy 3:1-7 - Qualifications for overseers/bishops
  • 1 Timothy 3:8-13 - Qualifications for deacons
  • 1 Timothy 5:17-20 - Elders who lead and preach should receive honor

Titus

  • Titus 1:5-9 - Appointing elders and desired qualifications

James

  • James 5:14 - Calling for elders to pray and anoint those who are sick

1 Peter

  • 1 Peter 5:1-4 - Instructions for elders to shepherd the flock and be examples

 

Other Verses about The Church

This section presents a collection of verses that offer additional insights into the nature and character of the Church. It describes the Church as a unified body with diverse members, a temple of the Holy Spirit, and the betrothed bride of Christ. These passages also emphasize the spread of the Gospel, the sanctity of individual believers, and the connections between various Christian communities in different regions.

  • Romans 12:4-5 - The church is one body with many members, each belonging to one another.
  • 1 Corinthians 3:16-17 - The church is the temple of God in which the Spirit dwells.
  • 1 Corinthians 6:19 - Individual Christians' bodies are also temples of the Holy Spirit.
  • 1 Corinthians 12:12-27 - Explains the unity and diversity of spiritual gifts in the church, which is Christ's body.
  • 2 Corinthians 11:2 - The church is betrothed to Christ as a pure bride.
  • Ephesians 5:23-32 - Christ is the head of the church, his bride, for whom he gave himself.
  • Philippians 3:6 - Paul persecuted the church before his conversion.
  • Colossians 4:16 - Paul's letters were circulated and read publicly in the churches.
  • 1 Thessalonians 1:1 - The church is comprised of those in God the Father and Jesus Christ.
  • 1 Thessalonians 2:14 - Suffering churches followed the pattern of the churches in Christ in Judea.

 

First uses of the word Catholic to describe The Church

The term "Catholic Church" to refer to the Christian Church appears to have been in use as early as the late first century or the beginning of the second century. One of the earliest known references to the "Catholic Church" is found in the writings of St. Ignatius of Antioch, who was a Christian bishop and martyr. St. Ignatius wrote a series of letters to various Christian communities on his way to martyrdom in Rome around 107-110 AD. In his letter to the Smyrneans, he wrote:

"Wherever the bishop appears, there let the people be; as wherever Jesus Christ is, there is the Catholic Church."
      -- (Letter to the Smyrneans, Chapter 8)

This quote indicates the use of the term "Catholic Church" to describe the universal Christian Church as a whole. It reflects the idea that the Church is not confined to a specific location but is present wherever the bishop, as a representative of the Church, is found. St. Ignatius' writings are among the earliest Christian documents outside the New Testament and provide valuable insights into the beliefs and organization of the early Christian communities.
