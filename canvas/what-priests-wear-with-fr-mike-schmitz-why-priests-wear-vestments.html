In this video, Fr. Mike Schmitz dives into the symbolic meaning of priestly garments, emphasizing how they go beyond mere decoration to reveal something profound: they hide the priest to reveal Jesus.

He starts with the alb, the white base layer, symbolizing purity and humility. It's like a blank canvas, ready for the transformation to come. Then, the stole, a long, thin scarf, comes into play. Fr. Mike describes it as a priestly "power tie," but one that signifies authority not for personal gain, but for service. It represents the power to preach, forgive, and act as Christ's representative.

Finally, the chasuble, the outer robe, takes center stage. Fr. Mike gets to the heart of the matter here: the chasuble hides the priest, concealing their individuality and imperfections. This purposeful concealment, he argues, allows Jesus to shine through. The priest becomes a vessel, a "little house" for Christ's presence and action in the world.

The symbolism doesn't stop there. Fr. Mike explores the meaning behind the changing colors of the vestments, how white reflects joy and purple ushers in solemnity. Each color choice becomes a visual cue, hinting at the spiritual undercurrent of the liturgical season.

Through this exploration, Fr. Mike invites viewers to see beyond the fabric and thread. He encourages us to appreciate the deliberate intention behind each garment, each color, each detail. These are not mere costumes, but tools that transform a priest into a conduit, a shepherd cloaked in humility, empowered to serve, and ultimately, to reveal the presence of Jesus himself.
