Our first meeting on Thursday was amazing!  And it will only get better from here!  

Here are some notes from the first week and info about the course materials:

  1. What you have signed up for is to inquire and learn about the Catholic Faith without any commitment. We will spend the next 8 lessons diving into fundamental topics as to what it means to be a Christian and more specifically a Catholic Christian.  This is a chance for you to dive into the Faith and go deeper and also for you to get your questions answered.  
  2. Please spend time preparing for each lesson.  In the "modules" will be the materials you need to dive in.  Please review the materials for each lesson as time allows.  We don't have books yet, but those will be coming soon.  The "Start Here" section are the most important materials to review prior to each week.  The "Go Deeper" section is available for you to go deeper as time allows.  Please always come to Thursday night class even if your time got away from you that week.  But please try and allocate some dedicated time to your day for working on this material.  I also will include a "Sacred Scripture Study" page each week to give you Scriptures you can use to dive into the Bible on our various topics.
  3. If you do plan on coming into communion with the Church, and you have been baptized, you will need to locate a baptismal certificate or some way to show you were baptized.  So please contact the Church where you were baptized and get some documentation if you don't already have something.  If you cannot obtain this, please ask Dax about your next steps.
  4. I encourage you to also find time to spend in PRAYER!  It could be going to some daily Mass at St. John Paul II (or your closest Catholic Church), it could be spending time with the Lord in Prayer and Bible Study.
  5. OK, I know this all may seem overwhelming!  But don't let it be... just take one step at a time.  Don't do it all at once, do it as you can.  Let the LORD lead you.  
  6. If you missed class, please answer these 2 questions to the best of your ability in your journal: (We will answer these again at the end of the RCIA process and compare to your first answers.  This is just for you, nothing to turn in :)  If your answer is "I have no clue" - that is perfectly fine :)
    1. What does it mean to be a Christian?
    2. What does it mean to be a Catholic?

God Bless us All on this journey together!

In Christ, Patrick
